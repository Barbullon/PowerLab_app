2. Testing Protocols:

A testing protocol is a systematic set of instructions for evaluating specific aspects of performance. Here, we outline three distinct protocols tailored to assess various dimensions of your pull-up capabilities.

Initiating the Test:
  • Before each test protocol, begin by hanging motionless for 2 seconds at full elbow extension.
Peak Power Test Protocol:
  • Execute an explosive pull-up, reaching to touch the wall above you. You are allowed to use legs and hips and a small swing as you are used to do in your training.
  • Allow a minimum of 3 minutes of rest if repeating the test.
  • If hands can't be released from the holds, continue the upward movement as high as possible.
Power Endurance Test Protocol:
  • Each repetition involves an explosive pull-up at maximum effort, emphasizing the rising phase. The descent can be deliberate but must extend until full elbow extension.
  • Rest for 3 seconds after each repetition before proceeding to the next. After two seconds, grab the bar, wait for 1 second and pull.
  • Conclude the test when unable to lift your chin over the bar.
Endurance Protocol:
  • Perform as many pull-ups as possible, focusing on energy conservation. Allow breaks between repetitions, ensuring no contact with the ground.
  • Conclude the test when unable to complete a full pull-up.
Hold type, additionnal weight:

An advantage of using your smartphone is that you can eveluate your performance on any type of holds. You can perform the tests with added weight too. And if a full pull-up is challenging, using a rubber band for assistance is permissible. But remember your setup when interpreting results. If you want to compare results, protocols have to be the identical

These protocols provide a structured approach to comprehensively assess your pull-up performance, ranging from explosive power to sustained endurance.
